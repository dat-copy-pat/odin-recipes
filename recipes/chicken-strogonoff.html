Chicken Strogowhat

Chicken strogonoff is a delicious and comforting dish perfect for a cozy night in. I wondered why some people call it Stroganoff, until I learned the dish, originated in Russia in the mid-19th century, was named after the very wealthy Stroganov family, which explains those, and possibly other spellings for this delicious dish.

Ingredients

  • Chicken, duh:Just 2 chicken breasts, cubed into bite-sized pieces.
  • Seasoning:A mixture of garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  • Condiments:Worcestershire sauce & Dijon mustard are ideal to bring tartness and heat.
  • Sour cream:Use full-fat sour cream for the richest flavor.
  • Onion and garlic:These two round out the sauce flavor perfectly.
  • Mushrooms:Cremini, button or portobello will do, just slice in small pieces.
  • Thyme:Thyme needs no explanation. Just add and enjoy.

Steps

  1. Cut the chicken into 1-inch pieces and place in a bowl. Sprinkle with seasoning powder, and then toss to coat evenly.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat with 2 tablespoons of oil. Add chicken and cook for 2-3 minutes per side. Leave it to brown and sear on one side before turning the pieces. Once cooked, transfer to a plate. Set aside and keep warm.
  3. Add butter to a hot pan, add the onion and mushrooms and sauté for 6-8 minutes.
  4. Add the Worcestershire sauce, mustard, and garlic. Cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Return the chicken to the pan and continue to cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is hot.
  6. Stir in the sour cream, then remove from the heat before the sauce starts to bubble, which can cause the sour cream to curdle.
  7. Season with additional salt and pepper, then serve with mashed potatoes or rice.
