Odin Recipes ft Chicken

Not really a recipe web page

This page is the final assignment in the CSS Foundations section of The Odin Project Foundations course.

The mission consists of styling a recipe page created in the HTML Foundations section. It wasn't very glamorous then, and it's not particularly flashy now, but it gave me a chance to review concepts and try stuff out.

I chose a mix of classic chicken recipes:

  • A wholesome soup that cures colds, unbreaks hearts, warms the chest, and sells books.
  • A classic, both French and Russian, that no one knows the right spelling for. Ask a Stroganov if you see one.
  • One that made the list because my partner and I cooked it on our first-date.
  • One that is not a typo, but a tribute to Taylor Swift's favorite recipe, taught to me by my 15-year-old Swiftie.

I didn't write out the actual recipes I use for these dishes, or take real pictures. So you'll just have to trust me when I say mine look a bit better than these photos, and I can surely style chicken better than recipe pages.

Anyways, here they are:

  • Chicken Soup
  • Strogonoff
  • Chicken Taynders
  • Pollo al Ajillo

Anyone who ever tried it knows that approaching The Odin Project is surely a brave endeavor. Yet, strangely enough, all the recipes in this project involve chicken. In the wise words of Alanis Morrissette: "Isn't it ironic, don't you think?".
